So...now programming?

As much as I learned and was fulfilled by my work in forensic toxicology, since I finished my degree in chemistry there was a nagging sense that it wasn’t where I belonged. I only began my pursuit of chemistry because a few friends were taking advanced classes in high school and I wasn’t sure what to pick. My path forward felt like an obligation. I was afraid to branch out, explore the unknown, and change. After a point I felt trapped. Was it too late to turn around and start over?

My breaking point was not getting the forensic toxicology job that I thought I had lined up before I made the move to Seattle. There weren’t many other options and the weight of my failure was rather devastating. But, it also meant I was free. Free to finally run down a new path and move towards a new goal. One that felt more my own. The flexibility, the job opportunities, the diverse career possibilities. With a few tools at my disposal, a few languages under my belt, and a passion for problem solving I can do anything. I can be anyone.
